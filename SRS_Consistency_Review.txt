[CRITICAL] Issue #1: iOS-only vs Cross-platform Conflict
---------------------------------------------------------
The document contradicts itself on supported platforms:

  Section 2.3 (User Classes):
    → General Public device: "Smartphone (iOS)"
    → Dishub Admin device: "Smartphone (iOS)"

  Section 2.5 (Operating Environment):
    → "Platforms: iOS"

  NFR-24 (Software Quality Attributes, p.11):
    → "The mobile app must run on Android 8.0 (API 26)
       or higher AND iOS 14.9 or higher"

  Product Scope:
    → "React Native / Expo" (cross-platform framework)

  Recommendation: Decide whether the app is iOS-only or
  cross-platform. If cross-platform, update Sections 2.3
  and 2.5. If iOS-only, remove Android from NFR-24 and
  reconsider using React Native.


[MAJOR] Issue #2: iOS Version Number Conflict
----------------------------------------------
  Section 2.5: "iOS 14 or higher"
  Section 3.2: "iOS 14 or higher"
  NFR-24:      "iOS 14.9 or higher"

  Note: iOS 14.9 does not exist. iOS versions go from
  14.8.1 directly to 15.0.

  Recommendation: Standardize to "iOS 14.0 or higher"
  across all sections.


[MAJOR] Issue #3: Camera Minimum Spec Conflict
------------------------------------------------
  Section 2.5 (Operating Environment):
    → "Camera: 8 MP or higher"
  NFR-26 (Camera Compatibility):
    → "cameras as low as 5 MP"

  Recommendation: Pick one. If you want broader device
  support, use 5 MP. Update Section 2.5 to match.

[CRITICAL] Issue #5: Dashboard Load Time Conflict
---------------------------------------------------
  NFR-03 in SRS document:
    → "within 1 second for initial load and 1 second
       for data refresh"
  NFR image:
    → "≤ 3 seconds initial load, ≤ 1 second refresh"

  Recommendation: 1 second for initial dashboard load is
  unrealistic for complex visualizations (heatmaps, graphs).
  Use 3 seconds for initial load, 1 second for refresh.


[CRITICAL] Issue #6: Uptime vs Downtime Math Error
----------------------------------------------------
  NFR-16 in SRS document states BOTH:
    → "99.9% of the time"
    → "Maximum downtime is about 3.6 hours per month"

  These contradict each other:
    99.9% uptime = ~43 minutes downtime/month
    3.6 hours downtime/month = 99.5% uptime

  Recommendation: Choose one. 99.5% with 3.6 hours/month
  is more realistic for this scale. Or use 99.9% and
  correct the downtime to ~43 minutes.


[CRITICAL] Issue #7: Rate Limiting Conflict
---------------------------------------------
  NFR-08 (Security Requirements):
    → "limit requests to 10 requests per minute per user"
  Business Rules (NFR-08):
    → "limited to 10 requests per minute"
  NFR image:
    → "Rate limiting: 100 req/min"

  10 req/min is extremely restrictive — a user opening the
  app and navigating through screens could hit that limit
  within seconds.

  Recommendation: Use 100 requests/minute for general API
  calls. Keep the 5 reports/day limit separately for
  report submissions.


[MAJOR] Issue #8: Data Growth Metrics Mismatch
------------------------------------------------
  NFR-27 in SRS document:
    → "10,000 users per day and 100,000 users per year"
  NFR image:
    → "≥ 10,000 officers"
    → "≥ 100,000 reports/year"
    → "≥ 500,000 scans/year"

  These measure completely different things (users vs
  officers vs reports vs scans).

  Recommendation: Clarify what is being measured and
  align both documents.

[CRITICAL] Issue #10: Payment Methods Conflict
-------------------------------------------------
  Product Scope (Section 1.4):
    → "scanning a government-issued QR code (centralized
       QRIS) or paying in cash"
    (Only 2 methods: QRIS + Cash)

  Section 4.3 (System Feature title):
    → "Integrated Digital Payment (QRIS/E-Wallet)"

  REQ-19 to REQ-21:
    → REQ-19: "QRIS payments"
    → REQ-20: "e-wallet payments"
    → REQ-21: "virtual account payments"
    (3 digital methods: QRIS + E-wallet + VA)

  Product Functions (Section 2.2):

       QRIS code" (QRIS only)

  Recommendation: Decide the actual payment methods.
  If only QRIS, remove REQ-20 and REQ-21. If multiple
  methods, update Product Scope and Product Functions.


[CRITICAL] Issue #11: Officer Role Contradiction
--------------------------------------------------
  Section 2.2 (Product Functions):
    → "Officers do not use the application"
  Section 2.3 (User Classes):
    → "They do not actively use the application"
    → "Device used: None"

  BUT NFR-15 (Safety Requirements):
    → "The officer's app must include a panic button"
  Business Rules (NFR-15):
    → "Only registered parking officers who have the
       official app (if provided) can use the panic button"

  If officers don't use the app, they can't have a
  panic button in the app.

  Recommendation: Either (a) give officers a minimal app
  with panic button functionality, or (b) remove the panic
  button requirement.
