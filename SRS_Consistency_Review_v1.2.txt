--------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE #1 — Officer Authentication Not Specified
--------------------------------------------------------------
Severity: MAJOR
Sections: 2.2 D, 2.3, NFR-13, NFR-17

PROBLEM:
NFR-13 (Admin authentication) states: "Admin and superadmin
logins must use bcrypt hashing and JWT tokens with expiry.
Session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity."

However, officer authentication is never specified. Officers
have their own app with access to:
  - Cash payment recording (handles financial data)
  - Emergency panic button (safety-critical function)

NFR-17 (RBAC) correctly defines four roles including "officer",
but the document does not specify HOW officers authenticate.
Without specified authentication:
  - Anyone could install the officer app and record fraudulent
    cash payments.
  - Anyone could trigger false panic alerts, wasting admin
    resources and undermining the safety system.

BR-09 (Session timeout) states: "Regular users have no session
timeout." It is unclear whether officers are categorized as
"regular users" or should have session management like admins.
Given that officers handle money and safety alerts, they should
not be treated the same as unauthenticated public users.

RECOMMENDATION:
Add an officer authentication requirement to NFR-13, e.g.:
  "Officer logins must use credentials issued by the admin
  during officer registration (Section 4.5). Officer sessions
  must use JWT tokens. Session timeout after 8 hours (one
  shift) of inactivity."

Also update BR-09 to clarify:
  "Admin auto-logout after 30 minutes inactivity. Officers
  auto-logout after 8 hours (one shift). Regular public users
  have no session timeout."

--------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE #2 — Offline Mode Has No Functional REQ in Section 4
--------------------------------------------------------------
Severity: MINOR
Sections: 2.4, NFR-32, Section 4.2

PROBLEM:
NFR-32 states: "App caches recent scan/validation data offline.
Reports can be saved locally and are automatically submitted
when connectivity is restored. The app must display a clear
offline indicator to the user."

Section 2.4 also mentions: "Offline mode available for cached
data and queued reports."

However, no functional requirement (REQ) in Section 4 specifies
the offline behavior. Specifically:
  - REQ-15 says "The system shall submit reports to the server"
    without mentioning offline queuing or local saving.
  - No REQ specifies caching validation data for offline use.
  - No REQ specifies displaying an offline indicator.

While NFR-32 describes the desired behavior, developers
typically look to functional REQs (Section 4) to know what to
build. Without matching REQs, offline mode may be overlooked
during implementation.

RECOMMENDATION:
Add offline-related REQs to Section 4.2 (or as a cross-cutting
concern), e.g.:
  REQ-xx: The system shall save reports locally when no internet
  connection is available.
  REQ-xx: The system shall automatically submit locally saved
  reports when internet connectivity is restored.
  REQ-xx: The system shall display a visible offline indicator
  when the device has no internet connection.


--------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE #3 — Cash Reconciliation Not Traced to a Specific REQ
--------------------------------------------------------------
Severity: MINOR
Sections: BR-04, Section 4.3, Section 4.7

PROBLEM:
BR-04 states: "Daily reconciliation report required."

However, no specific REQ implements daily cash reconciliation.
REQ-46 ("generate summary reports for a selected time period")
and REQ-47 ("print or export summary reports in PDF format")
are generic and could implicitly cover reconciliation, but
"reconciliation" specifically means comparing cash payments
recorded by officers against QRIS digital payments to ensure
all money is accounted for. This is a distinct financial
control function.

RECOMMENDATION:
Add a specific REQ to Section 4.7 or 4.4, e.g.:
  REQ-xx: The system shall generate a daily reconciliation
  report comparing officer-recorded cash payments against
  QRIS digital payments for each officer and zone.


--------------------------------------------------------------
ISSUE #4 — Priority Distribution Heavily Skewed to High
--------------------------------------------------------------
Severity: MINOR (recommendation, not an inconsistency)
Sections: 4.1 through 4.9

PROBLEM:
Of the 9 system features, 8 are classified as "High" priority
and 1 as "Low":

  4.1 QR Code Validation Scan       → High
  4.2 Report Illegal Parking         → High
  4.3 Integrated Digital Payment     → High
  4.4 Real-time Monitoring Dashboard → High
  4.5 Officer Account & Badge Mgmt   → High
  4.6 Payment History                → Low
  4.7 Report Management              → High
  4.8 Push Notifications & Alerts    → High
  4.9 System Administration          → High

When nearly all features share the same priority, the priority
classification loses its value for sprint planning and resource
allocation. Developers cannot determine which features to build
first if everything is "High."

This is not technically an inconsistency — the team may
intentionally consider all 8 features as equally essential. But
it weakens the document's utility for project management.

RECOMMENDATION:
Consider introducing a three-tier distinction to guide phased
development. For example:

  High (must-have for v1.0):
    4.1 QR Code Validation Scan
    4.2 Report Illegal Parking
    4.5 Officer Account & QR Badge Management

  Medium (important, build after core):
    4.3 Integrated Digital Payment (QRIS)
    4.4 Real-time Monitoring Dashboard
    4.7 Report Management
    4.8 Push Notifications & Emergency Alerts
    4.9 System Administration

  Low (future enhancement):
    4.6 Payment History

This is only a suggestion — the team should decide based on
stakeholder priorities and development capacity.
